Morocco

Location

North Africa

Form Of Government

Monarchy with two legislative houses, which means the political power is shared between the hereditary monarch, the House of Councilors and the House of Representatives

Leader

Maome VI

Population

36,91 millions

Capital and largest city

Rabat (capital) and Casablanca (largest city)

Monetary unit

Moroccan dirham

Languages

Arabic (spoken by two-thirds of the populations) and Tamazight (which became official language in 2011)

Religions

Islam (official state religion)

Economy

Exportation of raw materials, as well as tourism and telecommunications

Culture

Traditional domain of indigenous people called Imazighen

Curiosities

Rapidly modernizing but maintains a lot of its ancient architecture and traditional customs
