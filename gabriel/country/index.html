Saudi Arabia

Information about this country:

  • Location: Asia.
  • Form Of Government: monarchy.
  • Leader (president): Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud.
  • Population: 36655759 inhabitants.
  • Capital and largest city: Riad.
  • Monetary unit: Rial.
  • Languages: Arabic.
  • Religions: Islamic.
  • Economy: Saudi Arabia's economy is primarily based on the supply of oil and natural gas, in addition to religious tourism. According to the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC), Saudi Arabia holds 22.4% of the world's oil reserves.
  • Culture: the inhabitants of Saudi Arabia cannot eat pork or consume alcoholic beverages, as this substance is prohibited in the country. As Saudi Arabia has an authoritarian government, internet usage is controlled by it. In terms of clothing, the women wear long robes that cover their hair, so that only their faces are visible.
  • Curiosities: Saudi Arabia is home to the tallest building in the world, the Jeddah Tower, and cinemas are banned there. Its flag expresses the strength of the Islamic religion in the country, saying that there is no deity other than Allah. The country's legislation is based on Sharia and there is no constitution, as in Western countries. At 12 years of age, children are divided by gender in schools, and there are exclusive schools for boys and girls.
  • How many times has the country won the World Cup? Not once.
